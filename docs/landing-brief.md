# Landing Brief — Structured Intake Assistant

## Status

Draft v1

## Purpose

This document is the source of truth for the standalone marketing landing page for **Structured Intake Assistant**.

The landing page is a **product landing**, not a generic portfolio page.  
Secondarily, it should still work as a strong portfolio artifact that demonstrates product thinking, safety boundaries, UX clarity, and execution quality.

This brief exists to prevent generic output such as:

- vague “AI healthcare” positioning,
- fake medical dashboard aesthetics,
- random decorative visuals,
- unsafe claims,
- weak or confusing trust framing.

---

## Product Context

**Structured Intake Assistant** is a privacy-first, local-first primary care intake product.

It helps a patient prepare a **clear, structured, doctor-friendly summary** before a visit.

### Non-negotiable product boundaries

The product does **not**:

- diagnose,
- recommend treatment,
- provide triage / urgency advice,
- rank diseases.

AI, if present, must be framed as **optional** and requiring **explicit consent**.

---

## Landing Goal

### Primary goal

Explain the product idea clearly and credibly.

### Secondary goal

Drive users to:

- **View on GitHub**

### Current stage

Pre-release / early product stage.  
The landing page should feel real and polished, but should not pretend the product is already a mature commercial platform.

## Target Audience

### Primary audience

- Patients preparing for a primary care visit

### Secondary audience

- Doctors / clinicians who may understand the value of structured intake
- Recruiters / hiring managers / technical reviewers who may view the project as a portfolio-quality product artifact

### Audience priority rule

The page should speak **primarily to the patient in the hero and narrative flow**.

Clinical usefulness should be demonstrated as supporting proof, not as the main homepage voice.

---

## Core User Insight

Many patients do not feel properly heard during a doctor visit.

Common reasons:

- stress,
- difficulty explaining things clearly,
- forgetting important symptoms or history,
- presenting information in fragments,
- limited appointment time.

The product exists to reduce this communication gap by helping the patient arrive **prepared**.

---

## Core Job To Be Done

> Help a patient come to a doctor’s visit with a clear, structured summary instead of a stressed, fragmented explanation.

---

## Positioning

### Product framing

Structured Intake Assistant is a tool for preparing a **doctor-friendly summary before a visit**.

### It is not

- an AI doctor,
- a diagnosis assistant,
- a symptom checker,
- a treatment copilot,
- a clinical decision engine.

### Positioning statement

**Structured intake for primary care that helps patients organize symptoms, history, and context before an appointment.**

---

## Core Promise

The safest and strongest product promise is:

> Prepare a clear doctor-friendly summary before your visit.

This promise should remain central across the page.

### Supporting emotional framing

The product should acknowledge the real user frustration:

- it is hard to explain everything clearly under stress,
- important details are easy to forget,
- doctors have limited time,
- a structured handoff helps.

### Safe messaging rule

Do **not** promise:

- “correct diagnosis”,
- “better medical outcomes”,
- “your doctor will listen now”,
- “faster diagnosis”,
- “better treatment decisions”.

Prefer:

- “come prepared”,
- “organize what matters”,
- “generate a structured summary”,
- “make the handoff clearer”.

---

The page must repeatedly communicate:

- this is structured preparation, not diagnosis,
- this is doctor-friendly, not AI magic,

### Trust signals to emphasize

1. **Doctor-friendly summary**
2. **Private by default**
3. **Local-first**
4. **No diagnosis / no treatment recommendations**
5. **Optional AI only with explicit consent**
6. **Open-source**

- local-first,
- explicit consent for AI.

#### Format

---

### 3. Problem

#### Goal

Show that the product understands the user’s real frustration.

#### User problem narrative

At a doctor’s visit, it is easy to:

- forget details,
- explain symptoms out of order,
- lose context,
- feel rushed,
- leave feeling misunderstood.

#### Copy direction

The product should validate the user’s experience without blaming doctors.

#### Important framing rule

Do not use hostile “doctors don’t care” messaging.  
Prefer: appointments are short, stress is real, communication is hard.

---

### 4. Solution / Value Proposition

#### Goal

Show how the product responds to the problem.

#### Value proposition

Structured guidance helps the patient gather what matters before the visit and turn it into a concise summary for the doctor.

#### Key ideas

- guided intake
- organized symptom capture
- review before visit
- doctor-friendly output

---

### 5. How It Works

#### Goal

Make the product feel simple, not overwhelming.

#### Recommended step flow

1. Describe what brings you in
2. Add symptom details and relevant history
3. Review a structured summary
4. Print or export it for your visit

#### Rule

This section must make the flow feel easy and finite.

---

### 6. Doctor-Friendly Summary Showcase

#### Goal

Prove the main value of the product.

This is one of the most important sections on the page.

#### Must show

A clean structured output that feels quick to scan and clinically useful.

#### Key message

Instead of arriving with fragmented thoughts, the patient arrives with a clear summary.

#### Visual priority

High.  
The summary preview should be one of the strongest visual assets on the page.

---

### 7. Privacy & Safety

#### Goal

Reassure users that the product respects boundaries.

#### Must communicate

- privacy-first
- local-first
- no account
- no diagnosis
- no treatment recommendations
- no triage advice
- AI only with explicit consent

#### Tone

Clear and confident, not defensive.

---

### 8. Why It Feels Different

#### Goal

Contrast the product with the current messy reality.

#### Comparison direction

Without product:
- trying to remember everything,
- fragmented explanation,
- lost context,
- stress during the visit.

With product:
- guided preparation,
- organized context,
- structured summary,
- clearer handoff.

#### Rule

Keep this grounded and non-dramatic.

---

### 9. Open Source / Project Status

#### Goal

Be transparent about the current stage and give users a real next step.

#### Must communicate

- open-source project
- privacy-first by design
- product currently in active development
- GitHub is the main CTA for now

#### Rule

This section should increase credibility, not make the product feel unfinished.

---

### 10. Final CTA

#### Goal

End with a clean restatement of the product value.

#### Core direction

Repeat the preparation / clarity / structured handoff promise.

#### CTA

- View on GitHub
