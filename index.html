Problem

Appointments are short, and stress makes clear explanations harder.

Many people show up knowing something feels wrong, but not with a clean way to explain it. Important details get remembered too late, symptoms come out of order, and context is easy to lose in the moment.

Structured Intake Assistant is built for that gap: not to replace a doctor, but to help you arrive prepared.

What often gets lost

  • When symptoms started and how they changed
  • Relevant history, medications, or routines
  • Questions you meant to ask during the visit
  • The simple story that ties it all together

Solution

Guided preparation turns scattered notes into a clear handoff.

The product walks through symptoms, history, and context in a structured order, then turns that information into a concise summary designed for quick review before a primary care visit.

Guided intake

Prompts help you capture what brought you in without starting from a blank page.

Organized context

How it works

A simple four-step flow before your appointment.

The process is designed to feel finite and manageable, not like a complicated medical form.

1

Describe what brings you in

Start with the main concern in plain language.

2

Add symptom details and relevant history

Capture timing, severity, context, medications, and anything else worth remembering.

3

Review a structured summary

See the story organized into a format built for quick review.

4

Print or export it for your visit

Bring a cleaner handoff into the appointment instead of piecing it together under pressure.

Privacy & safety

Structured preparation, with clear boundaries.

The product is designed to stay within a narrow, safer scope: helping patients prepare information before a visit, not providing medical judgment.

Privacy posture

Private by default, local-first by design

  • Local-first workflow The primary experience stays focused on your device and your visit preparation.
  • No account required The core preparation flow is designed around direct use, not a signup funnel.
  • Open-source posture The product direction and trust boundaries are transparent and reviewable.

Safety boundaries

Clear about what the product does not do

  • No diagnosis It does not interpret symptoms as diseases or likely conditions.
  • No treatment recommendations It does not tell you what medication, test, or next step to pursue.
  • No triage or urgency advice It does not decide how urgent something is or where you should go.
  • Optional AI with explicit consent Any AI assistance stays secondary and only happens if you choose it.

Private by default

Bounded on purpose so the product stays easier to trust.
